A Benchmark For Continual Generative Modeling

Anonymous
Paper Code

Abstract

The ability to continually learn is an essential aspect of human cognition. However, it poses significant challenge to existing approaches in machine learning, which typically rely on identical distributions on both training and test datasets. While a large body of recent research has explored how to enable discriminative models to learn newly seen classes, the problem of enabling generative models to generate newly seen content has received substantially little attention. We closely analyze continual learning in the generative setting, and characterize it into two types, incremental generation, where we seek to sequentially generate new classes of images and compositional generation, which seeks to generate images with combinations of both newly and previously seen information. We provide a suite of benchmarks to assess continual performance in both settings and illustrate a set of baseline methods for achieving both goals.
